Elizabeth follows a user-centered approach in life and design.

"User-Centered Design

is a framework of processes (not restricted to interfaces or technologies) in which usability goals, user characteristics, environment, tasks and workflow of a product, service or process are given extensive attention at each stage of the design process. User-centered design can be characterized as a multi-stage problem-solving process that not only requires designers to analyze and envision the way users are likely to consume a product, but also to validate their assumptions with regard to the user behavior in real world tests. User-centered design tries to optimize the product around how users can, want, or need to use the product, rather than forcing the users to change their behavior to accommodate the product." | Wikipedia

Applying This

Elizabeth believes incredible user experiences are largely due to a designers understanding of the psychology of what people want. Designs have to come from a place of authenticity, open-mindedness, and creative problem-solving. Great designers are inventive and able to ask, "Why not?" over and over again. The same solution does not always work for different scenarios and sometimes the most important thing is to step back and take a look at the big picture. There might not be an ideal solution when everyone is working within the constraints of bad website architecture or a lack of user understanding. Great designers put the user first and work hard to understand his or her needs, desires, fears, struggles, and thoughts. Users are people, and the best designers can do is continually remember this.
