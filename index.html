Navigating the Wilderness

Showcasing skills in: UX Design

In applying for a job with Gaia, I want to showcase my ability to problem-solve and come up with some ideas that would help refine the app and make it very user-friendly. The UX part of UX|UI design is all about understanding users on a personal level. It's important to research, brainstorm, create a story, and then build, test, and refine based on real data. I have provided some visuals I created using various UX techniques. Because the purpose of this project is to showcase my UX skills, the research I did was primarily based off of my own personal thoughts and feelings and the thoughts and feelings of a couple close friends who use the app frequently.

Finding an Attorney

Showcasing skills in: Product Development & UX Design Thinking

After watching my family struggle to find important paperwork related to a loved one's estate, it became apparent that a simple process did not exist in the realm of estate-planning or in settling someone's estate. Initially I wanted to build a product designed to streamline the process of working with an estate-planning attorney. And throughout this journey, the vision of my product evolved to include a platform where value between clients and attorneys would be exchanged.

Planning a Leave of Absence

Showcasing skills in: Desktop UI Design

This project displays a flow of mockups leading a user to plan a leave of absence from work for a family health condition. Shanti Gupta is our user in this flow and she is planning a leave of absence for her daughter, Saavni Gupta. Shanti is planning her leave through an absence management portal set up for her through work. These mockups were created through a collaborative effort on a team of UX|UI designers. The team includes myself and two other individuals. It should be noted that logos have been removed and information has been made fake in an effort to protect secure information.

New Spotify Feature

Showcasing skills in: UX Design

I realized something needed to change when I shuffled one of my Spotify playlists and found that a bunch of songs that are split into parts, played out of order. I decided to design a feature to present to Spotify that would prevent these parts from shuffling when added to a shuffled playlist.

Applying This

Elizabeth believes incredible user experiences are largely due to a designers understanding of the psychology of what people want. Designs have to come from a place of authenticity, open-mindedness, and creative problem-solving. Great designers are inventive and able to pursue a design because it's what the user wants, not because it's what the business wants. The same solution doesn't always work for different scenarios and sometimes the most important thing is to take a step back and observe the big picture. Designers are psychologists in a way - to understand the user is the biggest priority. There might not be an ideal solution when everyone is working within the constraints of poor research, bad website architecture, or a lack of user understanding. Great designers put the user first and work hard to understand his or her needs, desires, fears, struggles, and thoughts. Users are people, and the best designers can do is continually remember this.

A Student of User Experiences

Elizabeth is continually curious and invested in learning and understanding users and their experiences. Her particular areas of interest include more than just designing experiences with technology. Below are a few additional ways she considers UX:

  • She has spent time studying architecture and interior design in order to apply the process of UX design to the process of moving into and living inside a home. Just as there are users of technology, we are all users of a home.
  • She loves to experiment with food while cooking. She has recently begun brainstorming how UX can be applied to the art of everything having to do with food. We all know there's an art to preparing how food looks on a plate, but the experience extends beyond the way it looks visually.
  • She is always curious about how things work. She is very mechanically-minded, has keen spatial abilities, and loves to fix things. All of this draws her to the art of developing physical products. Elizabeth is currently working on a particular chalk bag design idea for rock climbing.
