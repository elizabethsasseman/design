I knew there had to be a way to keep songs with multiple parts together even when a playlist is shuffled. Let me explain what I mean by songs with multiple parts. Check out the Spotify screenshots below:

I used an example of a classic song, Fly Like An Eagle by Steve Miller Band. You can see that Space Intro and Fly Like An Eagle are split into two parts, even though together they make up one very popular song. My guess is that there are a lot of people who don't care to listen to the Space Intro part of Fly Like An Eagle every time they listen to the song, so it make sense Spotify has kept these parts separate. But if someone - who does want to listen to both parts together as the complete song that it is - adds both parts to a playlist they intend on shuffling, I'm certain most people like me will be frustrated when both parts don't play together or in the right order.

There are a lot of songs split into multiple parts like this that I've found on Spotify. My goal was to design a feature that would allow users the option to play these parts together as one song and in the right order, even when a playlist is shuffled.

Brainstorm

I wanted to design a feature that would function seamlessly with Spotify's current features. I also wanted to allow users the choice to not link, link, unlink or re-link parts as often or little as they wanted. I knew allowing as much flexibility as possible would be necessary in order to please each type of user. Below are some initial questions I began considering:

  • Should there be a limit in the number of parts that can be linked? What would happen if someone tried to link thousands of songs?
  • Should it be possible to unlink one part at a time or would unlinking one part unlink all parts?
  • Does each linked part need to be on the same playlist or album in order to play as a linked group of parts?
  • Will parts remain linked if the playlist these parts are on is deleted?
  • Are linked parts on a collaborative playlist linked for all users who have access to that playlist? Should that option exist?
  • If three parts are linked and on a shuffled playlist, will they exist in the randomized queue once or three times?

Discuss & Research

I described my idea to several friends and asked if they thought the ability to link parts of songs in Spotify would be useful. Everyone felt like it would be a beneficial feature, but most of them hadn't actually run into a situation where they wished the ability to link parts existed. I decided to create a poll in one of my Instagram stories to get a few more responses. Below are screenshots of my Instagram poll:

Based on my poll, 86% of the 22 people who responded feel like the ability to link parts of songs should be possible.

There are over 140 million active monthly Spotify users, according to a simple Google search. Although my poll reached an extremely small sample of this population, I wanted to design this feature for fun, regardless of how many Spotify users shared in my frustration. My plan was to post my idea on Spotify's Ideas Submissions page and see what sort of feedback it would get.

Ideas Submissions

After posting my idea to Spotify's Ideas Submissions page, I decided to browse around and see whether any other users had come up with the same idea as me. Sure enough, one other user had submitted a similar idea in 2015. And about a year ago, someone from Spotify commented on this user's post saying that this idea would go into the "Not Right Now" pile because they had no plans to implement it, but that it might be up for consideration eventually.
